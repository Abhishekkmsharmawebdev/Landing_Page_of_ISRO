About Chandrayaan-3

Chandrayaan-3 History

September 2021, Chandrayaan-3 was a proposed lunar mission by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It aimed to build upon the lessons learned from Chandrayaan-2, focusing on achieving a successful soft landing on the Moon. Chandrayaan-3 was intended to consist of a lander and rover, similar to Chandrayaan-2, but with improvements to ensure a successful landing. However, specific details and a launch timeline were yet to be officially announced by ISRO. Please check the latest news and updates for the most current information on Chandrayaan-3.

ABOUT ISRO

The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) is India's premier space agency, founded in 1969. It has achieved significant milestones in space exploration, including launching numerous satellites for communication, navigation, and Earth observation. ISRO's notable achievements include the successful Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) and the Chandrayaan-2 lunar mission. It is known for its cost-effective approach to space missions and has earned global recognition for its contributions to space science and technology. ISRO continues to play a crucial role in advancing India's space capabilities and international collaborations in space exploration.
